<h2>What is the Average Temperature of a Campfire?</h2>

<h2>What Affects the Heat of a Campfire?</h2>

The temperature of a campfire is affected by several factors, including the type of fuel being used, the size of the fire, and the amount of oxygen available. 

<h3>Type of Fuel</h3>

The type of fuel used to build a campfire will affect the temperature. Hardwoods such as oak, hickory, and maple will burn hotter than softer woods such as pine and cedar. Additionally, some types of fuel such as charcoal or lighter fluid can help to increase the temperature. 

<h3>Size of the Fire</h3>

The size of the fire can also affect the temperature. A larger fire will typically burn hotter than a smaller one, as more fuel is being burned and there is more oxygen available. 

<h3>Amount of Oxygen</h3>

The amount of oxygen available will also affect the temperature of a campfire. If there is not enough oxygen, the fire will not be able to burn as hot as it could with an adequate amount of oxygen.
