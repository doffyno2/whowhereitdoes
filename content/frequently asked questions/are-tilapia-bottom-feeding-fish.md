Tilapia are a popular and widely available type of fish known for their mild flavor and relatively low cost. But are they bottom feeders? The answer is, it depends on the species. Some tilapia are bottom feeders, while others are not. In this article, we'll explore the differences between bottom-feeding and non-bottom-feeding tilapia, as well as how to tell the difference between the two.

<h2>What Is Bottom Feeding?</h2>

Bottom feeding is a type of feeding behavior in which a fish feeds on the bottom of the body of water. Bottom feeders typically eat small particles of organic matter from the bottom of the water, such as algae, plankton, and small insects. Bottom feeders also eat detritus, which is the waste material from other organisms in the water.

<h2>How Can I Tell If a Tilapia Is a Bottom Feeder?</h2>

The easiest way to tell if a tilapia is a bottom feeder is to look at the shape of its body. Bottom feeders tend to have a flattened, elongated body shape, while non-bottom feeders tend to have a more rounded body shape. 

Another way to tell if a tilapia is a bottom feeder is to look at its color. Bottom feeders tend to be darker in color, while non-bottom feeders tend to be lighter in color.

Bottom feeders play an important role in the aquatic ecosystem.
