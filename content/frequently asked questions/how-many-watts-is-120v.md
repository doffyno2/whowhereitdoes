<h2>What Are Watts?</h2>

Watts are a unit of power, or the rate at which energy is transferred. The watt is named after the Scottish engineer James Watt, who invented the steam engine. Watts are commonly used to measure the output of appliances, such as microwaves, televisions, and electric stoves.

<h2>What Are Volts?</h2>

Volts, also known as electrical potential, are a measure of the pressure of an electric current. The volt is named after the Italian physicist Alessandro Volta, who invented the first battery. Volts are commonly used to measure the voltage of batteries and the voltage of electrical outlets.

<h2>What Is the Power Equation?</h2>

The power equation is a mathematical formula that is used to calculate the power in watts of an electric circuit. The equation is P = V x I, where P is power in watts, V is voltage in volts, and I is current in amperes (amps). This equation can be used to calculate the number of watts in a circuit when the voltage and current are known.
