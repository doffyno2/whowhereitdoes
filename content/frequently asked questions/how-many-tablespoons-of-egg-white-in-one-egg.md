<h2>What is an Egg White?</h2>

An egg white is the clear, viscous liquid found inside a chicken egg. It is made up of mostly water, with a small amount of proteins, fats, and carbohydrates. The egg white is often used in cooking and baking, and it can also be consumed raw. Egg whites are often separated from the yolk to create lighter dishes, such as meringues and angel food cake. 

<h2>What Are the Nutritional Benefits of Egg Whites?</h2>

Egg whites are a great source of protein, with one large egg providing around 4g of protein. They are also low in fat and calories, making them an excellent addition to any healthy diet. Egg whites are also an excellent source of vitamins and minerals, including Vitamins A, D, and B12, and minerals such as zinc and selenium. 

<h2>How Many Tablespoons of Egg White in One Egg?</h2>

The amount of egg white in one large egg varies, but a good rule of thumb is that one large egg contains approximately two tablespoons of egg white. This is equivalent to approximately 30ml or 1.5 fluid ounces of egg white.

<h2>Tips for Separating Egg Whites and Yolks</h2>

Separating egg whites and yolks can be a tricky process, but it is made easier with the right tools. The most common way to separate egg whites and yolks is to use an egg separator. This is a small plastic or metal device that fits over the top of an egg and allows you to easily pour the whites out while keeping the yolk intact.

Another easy way to separate egg whites and yolks is to crack open the egg and carefully pour the contents into your hand. Then, gently push the yolk back and forth between your hands to separate it from the whites. This method is messier than the egg separator but can still be done easily. 

<h2>How to Use Egg Whites in Cooking</h2>

Egg whites are used in a variety of different dishes and can be cooked in a variety of ways. They can be beaten into a fluffy meringue for pies and desserts, or whipped into a stiff foam for Angel Food cake. Egg whites can also be cooked on their own and used as a light protein source in dishes such as omelets. 

<h2>What Are Alternatives to Egg Whites?</h2>

If you are looking for an alternative to egg whites, there are several options available. Aquafaba is a popular vegan alternative that is made from the liquid in canned chickpeas. It can be used as a substitute for egg whites in a variety of recipes and has a similar texture and consistency. Other vegan alternatives include ground flaxseed meal, silken tofu, and store-bought vegan egg replacers.
