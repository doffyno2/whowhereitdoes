<h2>What Is a Fluid Ounce?</h2>

A fluid ounce is a unit of measurement for liquids. It is equal to about 29.57 milliliters (mL). This is the amount of liquid that can be contained in a standard glass, bottle, or cup. The fluid ounce is a US customary unit of measurement and is not used in other countries.

<h2>What Is the Maximum Number of Fluid Ounces You Can Travel With?</h2>

The maximum number of fluid ounces you can travel with depends on the type of liquid and where you are traveling. When traveling domestically within the US, the Transportation Security Administration (TSA) allows passengers to bring a limited amount of liquids, aerosols, gels, creams, and pastes in their carry-on bags. These items must be in containers that are 3.4 ounces or less and must be placed in a quart-sized, clear, plastic bag. This bag must be placed in the screening bin for X-ray screening.

International travelers should check with their airline for the maximum number of fluid ounces they can bring with them. Generally, the maximum amount of liquids allowed is limited to 3.4 ounces or 100 milliliters.

<h2>What Are Some Examples of Liquids That Are Allowed?</h2>

The TSA allows travelers to bring a limited amount of liquids in their carry-on bags. Examples of allowed liquids include water, juice, and other beverages, as well as shampoo, conditioner, lotion, and other toiletries. Soups, syrups, and other liquid foods are also allowed, as long as they are in containers that are 3.4 ounces or less.

<h2>What Liquids Are Not Allowed?</h2>

The TSA does not allow travelers to bring any liquids that are flammable or combustible in their carry-on bags. This includes items such as paint, gasoline, matches, and lighter fluid. Aerosol cans are also not allowed, as they could potentially be used as a bomb.

<h2>Are There Exceptions to the Fluid Ounce Limit?</h2>

Yes, there are exceptions to the fluid ounce limit when traveling. Medications and baby formula are allowed in larger containers, as long as they are declared at the security checkpoint. The container must be larger than 3.4 ounces, and the traveler must provide proof that the item is a medication or baby formula.

If you have a liquid that is too big to bring on your flight, you have a few options. You can put the item in your checked baggage, or you can try to find a way to make the item smaller. Many airports have refill stations where you can purchase smaller containers of your items. You can also transfer the item to a smaller container, such as a travel-sized container.
