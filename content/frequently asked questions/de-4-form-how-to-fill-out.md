Filling out forms can be a tedious and time-consuming task, but it's an important part of life. Whether you're applying for a job or a loan, or filing taxes, it's important to know what you're doing. This article will go over the four most common forms and tell you how to fill them out correctly. 

<h2>W2 Form</h2>

The W2 form is an important form used to report an employee's wages and taxes withheld. It's used by employers to report income and taxes withheld to the Internal Revenue Service (IRS). To fill out the W2 form, you'll need the following information:

<ul>
<li>Your social security number</li>
<li>Your employer's name and address</li>
<li>Your employer's federal identification number</li>
<li>Your wages for the year</li>
<li>Federal income tax withheld</li>
<li>State income tax withheld</li>
<li>Local income tax withheld</li>
<li>Social security wages</li>
<li>Social security tax withheld</li>
<li>Medicare wages and tips</li>
<li>Medicare tax withheld</li>
<li>Any other withholding</li>
</ul>

Once you have all the information, you can fill out the W2 form. Make sure to double-check your entries and confirm that all the information is correct.

<h2>1040 Form</h2>

The 1040 form is used to file your personal federal income tax return. To fill out the 1040 form, you'll need the following information:

<ul>
<li>Your social security number</li>
<li>Your filing status (single, married filing jointly, etc.)</li>
<li>Your total income for the year</li>
<li>Your deductions and credits</li>
<li>Your total tax liability</li>
<li>Your total payments for the year</li>
<li>Your total refund or amount you owe</li>
<li>Your bank account information, if you're expecting a refund</li>
</ul>

Once you have all the information, you can fill out the 1040 form. Make sure to double-check your entries and confirm that all the information is correct.

<h2>W4 Form</h2>

The W4 form is used by employers to withhold the correct amount of federal income tax from an employee's wages. To fill out the W4 form, you'll need the following information:

<ul>
<li>Your social security number</li>
<li>Your filing status (single, married filing jointly, etc.)</li>
<li>Your total income for the year</li>
<li>Your deductions and credits</li>
<li>Your total allowances for the year</li>
<li>Any additional amount you wish to have withheld from your wages</li>
</ul>

Once you have all the information, you can fill out the W4 form. Make sure to double-check your entries and confirm that all the information is correct.

<h2>I-9 Form</h2>

The I-9 form is used to verify the identity and employment eligibility of an employee. To fill out the I-9 form, you'll need the following information: 

<ul>
<li>Your full name</li>
<li>Your date of birth</li>
<li>Your social security number</li>
<li>Your address</li>
<li>Your citizenship status</li>
<li>Your work authorization document information (if applicable)</li>
<li>Your employer's name and address</li>
</ul>

Once you have all the information, you can fill out the I-9 form. Make sure to double-check your entries and confirm that all the information is correct.
